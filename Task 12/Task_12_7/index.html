Piping and plumbing fittings...

Pipes are commonly joined by welding, using threaded pipe and fittings; sealing a connection with a pipe thread compound, or PTFE string, or by using a mechanical coupling. Process piping is usually joined by welding using a TIG or MIG process. The most common process pipe joint is the butt weld. The ends of pipe to be welded must have a certain weld preparation called an End Weld Prep which is typically at an angle of 37.5 degrees to accomodate the filler weld metal.

Cleaning...

The inside of pipes can be cleaned with a tube cleaning process, if they are contaminated with debris of fouling. This depends on the process that the pipe will be used for and the cleanliness needed for the process. In some cases the pipes are cleaned using a displacement device formally known as a Pipeline Inspection Gauge or "pig".
